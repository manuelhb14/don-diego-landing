# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

The primary users are qualified prospective buyers, homeowners, and investors considering a residence or long-term relationship with Don Diego in San Miguel de Allende. They are evaluating more than a property: they are deciding whether the place, community, services, landscape, and ownership experience feel complete enough to merit a private conversation or visit.

Secondary users include future residents and owners who will rely on service-oriented tools such as reservations, guest access, farm orders, rental coordination, calculators, buyer guidance, and contact flows. Those surfaces should still feel like Don Diego, but the default design register for the repo is the public brand experience.

## Product Purpose

Don Diego Club Residencial presents a mixed-use residential development in San Miguel de Allende built around four connected components: Club Residencial, Organic Farm & Flowers, Wellness Center, and Presa de la Cantera. The site should make the project feel specific, credible, complete, and worth visiting in person.

Success means a qualified visitor understands the breadth of the project, senses that it is not a generic real-estate development, and feels confident enough to explore properties, review the project components, contact the team, or schedule a private visit.

## Brand Personality

Modern, complete, smart.

The brand should feel contemporary without losing warmth, comprehensive without becoming heavy, and intelligent without sounding corporate. The emotional target is calm confidence: a visitor should feel that Don Diego has a clear point of view, a mature operating vision, and a deep relationship to land, community, and San Miguel.

## Anti-references

Do not make Don Diego look like a common, boring page for a new real-estate project.

Avoid generic luxury-development templates: oversized render heroes with vague slogans, interchangeable amenity card grids, beige brochure layouts, stock-like lifestyle imagery, hollow exclusivity language, fake minimalism, and repetitive section structures that could belong to any residential launch.

Avoid treating San Miguel, wellness, sustainability, or community as decorative keywords. These ideas must be grounded in concrete project details, real place cues, imagery, architecture, services, and the actual mixed-use ecosystem.

## Design Principles

1. Make the place unmistakable. Every page should carry signals of Don Diego, San Miguel, the land, water, agriculture, architecture, and the four-component ecosystem.
2. Show completeness without clutter. The project is broad, so structure information with editorial discipline, clear hierarchy, and confident pacing.
3. Convert through confidence, not pressure. Calls to contact or schedule a visit should feel natural after the story, details, imagery, and proof have done their work.
4. Let tools feel like service. Product-like surfaces should be quiet, useful, and polished, supporting the residential experience rather than becoming a separate app aesthetic.
5. Reject category reflexes. Future design work should actively avoid generic real-estate patterns and choose layouts, copy, imagery, and motion that feel specific to Don Diego.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target sensible WCAG AA practice for public-facing pages: readable contrast, keyboard-accessible controls, useful alt text, predictable focus states, semantic structure, and clear bilingual Spanish and English content. Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` for animated and scroll-linked experiences. No additional accessibility requirements were specified.
